Total revenue-- $695 million, representing 27% year-over-year growth and exceeding the high end of the company's own guidance by 4%.

Atlas revenue-- Grew 29% year over year, surpassed the $2 billion run rate mark, and contributed a record $114 million in net new revenue for the quarter, accounting for 72% of total revenue, up from 71%.

Non-Atlas revenue-- Increased 20% year over year, producing the best quarterly growth in the past two years.

Customer count-- Ended with over 65,200 customers, adding 2,700 in the quarter, and achieved a 60% full-year increase in additions.

Operating margin-- Reported at 23%, an improvement from 21% a year ago, with positive GAAP operating income for the quarter.

Net income-- $143 million, or $1.65 per share based on 86.5 million diluted shares, up from $108 million and $1.28 per share on 84.6 million diluted shares last year.

Total company net ARR expansion rate-- Reached 121%, increasing from 120% last quarter and 119% a year prior.

Non-Atlas ARR-- Grew 13% year over year, excluding duration impact, with increased momentum from financial services, public sector, and technology customers.

RPO (remaining performance obligations)-- Rose to $1.47 billion from $748 million, a 97% year-over-year growth.

Large deals closed-- Included an approximately $90 million Atlas deal with a major tech company and a greater than $100 million Enterprise Advanced (EA) deal with a large financial institution, noted as the largest total contract value in company history.

Customers generating $100,000+ ARR-- 2,799 customers;customers generating $1 million+ ARR-- 402 customers. (Management noted rapid growth in these cohorts, but the 1,726% figure appears to be a transcript error and is omitted for accuracy.)

Feature usage in Atlas-- Of Atlas customers producing $100,000+ ARR, 44% use two or more platform features, rising from 36% prior year.

Operating cash flow-- $180 million;free cash flow-- $177 million, comparing favorably to $51 million and $23 million, respectively, last year.

Cash, equivalents, and investments-- Nearly $2.4 billion at quarter-end.

Atlas revenue guidance-- Projected growth of approximately 26% in Q1 and 21%-23% for fiscal 2027.

Non-Atlas revenue guidance-- Expected mid to upper single-digit growth in Q1 and low to mid single-digit growth for fiscal 2027.

Total fiscal 2027 revenue guidance-- Anticipated at $2.86 billion to $2.9 billion, equating to 16%-18% growth.

Fiscal 2027 operating margin guidance-- ~19.5% (non-GAAP) at the high end of guidance; commitment to expanding operating margin by 100 basis points while investing for growth.

Capital allocation-- 100% of free cash flow planned for share repurchases and settling employee RSU tax obligations in fiscal 2027; $55 million spent this quarter repurchasing 133,000 shares.

AI product uptake metrics-- The number of customers using vector search and Voyage embedding models nearly doubled year over year.

Leadership changes-- Erica Woolini appointed as Chief Customer Officer, effective March 3; Cedric Pash and Paul Kepambesis to depart, with a new CRO search in late stages and leadership transition planned through Q2.

MongoDB(MDB+2.97%)reported accelerated growth across both Atlas and non-Atlas product lines, highlighted by all-time record new ARR and substantial large enterprise deals in regulated industries. Management indicated broad-based demand from AI native, fintech, public sector, and Fortune 500 cohorts, with specific expansion in AI workloads and deeper adoption of platform features among high-value Atlas customers. Strategic execution included a significant increase in RPO, prioritization of feature parity for EA with Atlas, and a leadership transition designed to support expanded go-to-market initiatives, especially in the AI and enterprise segments.

CEO Desai stated, "MongoDB's foundation is in great shape, and the company is well on its way to become the generational data platform of choice in the AI and multi-cloud era."

New customer activity was sustained by self-service motions, in addition to upmarket sales, with record new ARR driven by EMEA team performance at major financial institutions, retailers, and technology companies.

Large bundled Atlas and EA deals materially benefited non-Atlas results, while Atlas revenue attribution was affected by one unusually large transaction where "we had to attribute more of that revenue to EA versus Atlas," according to Berry.

On-premises deployment demand increased with EA positioned as mission critical among major financial, telecom, and government customers; multiple references emphasized customer insistence on operational resilience and hybrid infrastructure.

CFO Berry confirmed expectations for 80%-100% operating cash conversion in fiscal 2027, in line with long-term company targets.

AI contributions to revenue remain nascent, but the customer base adopting agentic and AI-related database features grew rapidly.

Industry glossary

Atlas: MongoDB's fully managed cloud database service, providing multi-cloud deployment and integrated features such as search, vector search, and embeddings.

Enterprise Advanced (EA): The company’s commercial, on-premises database offering designed for regulated enterprise workloads, supporting hybrid environments.

Vector search: A database capability allowing for similarity search on high-dimensional datasets, critical for AI and machine learning applications.

Voyage: MongoDB's embedding model technology, enabling deeper integration of AI/machine learning within customer applications.

ARR (annual recurring revenue): A metric representing the predictable and recurring revenue components generated from customer contracts over a one-year period.

RPO (remaining performance obligations): The total contracted revenue not yet recognized on the balance sheet, reflecting future revenue under existing agreements.
