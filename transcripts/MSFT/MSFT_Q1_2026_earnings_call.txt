Azure capacity constraints— Amy E. Hood stated, "We now expect to be capacity constrained through at least the end of our fiscal year, with demand exceeding current infrastructure build-out, resulting in lost revenue opportunities for Azure in fiscal Q1 2026."

Cloud gross margin compression— Amy E. Hood noted, "Microsoft cloud gross margin percentage should be roughly 66%, down year over year, driven by the continued investments in AI as well as the mix shift to Azure," explicitly flagging headwinds to profitability.

Volatility in other income— "The combination of OpenAI's conversion to a public benefit corp and the ongoing nature of our partnership will result in increased volatility," according to Amy E. Hood, in other income and expense.

Gaming revenue decline— "In gaming, revenue decreased 3% in constant currency against a strong prior year comparable," with further declines expected next quarter due to difficult prior-year comparisons.

Total revenue-- $77.7 billion, up 17% in constant currency, reflecting broad growth across all major segments.

Cloud revenue-- $49.1 billion, up 25% in constant currency, with cloud gross margin at 68%, down year over year due to AI investments.

Commercial bookings-- Up 112% (111% in constant currency), driven by Azure commitments from OpenAI and an increase in $100 million-plus contracts.

Commercial remaining performance obligation (RPO)-- $392 billion, up 51% year over year, with nearly double the balance over the past two years and a weighted average duration of approximately two years.

Operating income-- Up 22% in constant currency, with company-level operating margins at 49% and improvement in high-margin businesses.

Earnings per share-- $4.13, up 21% in constant currency when adjusted for OpenAI investment impacts.

Capital expenditures-- $34.9 billion, split between short-lived assets (primarily GPUs/CPUs) and long-lived assets, with $11.1 billion allocated to finance leases for large data center sites.

Free cash flow-- $25.7 billion, up 33%, driven by strong cloud billings and collections, partially offset by higher supplier payments.

Productivity and business processes segment revenue-- $33 billion, up 14% in constant currency, led by Microsoft 365 Copilot and growth in both ARPU and seats.

Intelligent cloud segment revenue-- $30.9 billion, up 27% in constant currency, with Azure and other cloud services revenue up 39% in constant currency.

Azure AI services revenue-- Demand exceeded available supply for the quarter; capacity additions ongoing.

LinkedIn revenue-- Up 9% in constant currency, with talent solutions impacted by a weak hiring market.

Gaming-- Segment revenue decreased 3% in constant currency due to strong prior-year comparables; Xbox content and services revenue up 1%.

Q2 outlook-- Revenue guidance of $79.5 billion to $80.6 billion (14%-16% growth) for fiscal Q2 2026; expects commercial cloud gross margin compression, higher capital expenditures, and capacity constraints to persist through fiscal year-end.

OpenAI partnership-- A new definitive agreement was closed, extending exclusive rights for Azure until AGI or through 2030 and product/model IP rights through 2032; OpenAI has contracted an incremental $250 billion of Azure services, not reflected in current results.

AI platform momentum-- Satya Nadella stated, "Microsoft Cloud revenue surpassed $49 billion, up 26% year over year, and our commercial RPO grew over 50% to nearly $400 billion with a weighted average duration of only two years."

Microsoft(MSFT+5.25%)reported accelerating demand for cloud and AI services, highlighted by triple-digit commercial bookings growth and a record RPO balance concentrated in short-duration contracts. Management emphasized ongoing, material investments in infrastructure and talent to scale AI capabilities, with Azure expected to remain capacity constrained through at least the end of the fiscal year. The company finalized a new, long-term agreement with OpenAI for Azure exclusivity and expanded IP rights, driving incremental contracted cloud demand not yet recognized in reported revenue or bookings.

Satya Nadella said, "We have 900 million monthly active users of our AI features across our products." There are over 150 million monthly active users of first-party Copilots, illustrating rapid adoption of AI-driven product enhancements.

Amy E. Hood stated, "Roughly half of our spend was on short-lived assets, primarily GPUs and CPUs to support increasing Azure platform demand, growing first-party apps, and AI solutions," emphasizing a shift toward matching asset lives with contract durations.

Company gross margin percentage was 69%, down slightly year over year, driven by investments in AI.

Management described current investments and capacity expansion as tightly correlated with "booked business today," according to Amy E. Hood, rather than speculative future demand, signaling a disciplined approach to growth despite ongoing capacity constraints.

Commercial contract risk exposure was discussed, with large, concentrated Azure deals accompanied by short-term revenue consumption cycles and asset allocation guardrails to manage customer and product mix.

Industry glossary

Commercial remaining performance obligation (RPO): The total value of contracted, yet-to-be-recognized revenue from commercial customers, typically reported with weighted average contract duration.

ARPU: Average revenue per user; key for analyzing revenue growth in subscription-based products.

Finance lease: A lease arrangement in which Microsoft records the asset on its balance sheet, commonly used for data center sites and related infrastructure.

Agentic AI / Copilot: Microsoft's AI-driven productivity systems and features facilitating workflow automation and task orchestration within organizational tools.

Tokens per dollar per watt: An internal Microsoft metric for measuring AI compute efficiency in relation to cost and power consumption.

Fungible fleet: A flexible, globally distributed Microsoft data center infrastructure, able to allocate compute and storage resources across first-party and third-party workloads dynamically.
