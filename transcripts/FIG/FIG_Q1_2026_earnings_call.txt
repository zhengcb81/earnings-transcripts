Revenue-- $333 million, up 46% year over year, with growth accelerating for two consecutive quarters.

Net dollar retention rate-- Reached 139%, a 3 percentage point increase from the prior quarter, the highest in over two years.

International revenue growth-- International business revenue grew 48% year over year, outpacing overall company growth.

Operating margin-- Non-GAAP operating margin stood at 16%, with operating income of $52 million.

Free cash flow-- $89 million in free cash flow and a margin of 27%; Q1 margin was reduced by a $56 million annual bonus payout, which had a 17 percentage point impact.

Paid customer base-- Expanded to approximately 690,000, increasing 54% year over year.

Large customer segments-- Paid customers with more than $10,000 in ARR grew 37%, while those over $100,000 grew 48%, both reflecting acceleration.

AI credit monetization-- AI credit monetization began March 18; over 75% of Org and Enterprise plan users who exceeded previous limits continued to consume credits, and more than 95% of those users remained active by end of April.

AI-driven usage and expansion-- Among customers with more than $100,000 in ARR, weekly usage of Make reached 60%, up from just over 50% last quarter.

MCP adoption-- Customers with more than $100,000 in ARR that used MCP grew full seats approximately 70% faster over the quarter than those not using MCP.

Guidance update-- Full-year revenue guidance was raised by $55 million to a range of $1.422 billion-$1.428 billion, implying 35% growth at midpoint; non-GAAP operating income guidance raised by $25 million to $125 million-$135 million.

Cash position-- Ended the quarter with $1.6 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities.

Product metrics-- In Q1, 60% of Figma’s largest customers used Make weekly, and over 80% of Make users on Full Seats continued using Figma Design.

AI-driven Pro team conversions-- Pro team conversions to paid plans increased over 150% year over year, led by adoption of AI capabilities.

Figma(FIG+9.19%)reported accelerating revenue growth, significant expansion in its paid customer base, and early success in monetizing AI features across its platform. New credit monetization initiatives are showing strong engagement, particularly among enterprise and organizational users who continue to purchase additional credits after enforcement. The company emphasized unique competitive positioning through platform differentiation and ongoing product expansions, while its raised full-year guidance reflects confidence in broad-based seat growth, retention, and continued AI-driven upsell. Figma’s updated bonus accrual structure will continue to impact quarterly free cash flow margins moving forward.

A major international hyperscaler unified over 35,000 paid seats under a single agreement, representing one of the largest deals in company history.

Field said, "Adoption of Weave at NBBJ is expected to triple in the next 3 months," highlighting growing traction with AI-enabled products beyond traditional software design.

Melwani noted, "Pro teams that purchase AI credit add-ons had more seats per team and an average annualized spend of over 3x than that of teams that haven't purchased add-ons."

The launch of full seat AI credit limits now ties revenue directly to increasing AI usage on the platform, establishing a new monetization milestone.

Industry glossary

Make: Figma’s AI-powered collaborative design tool enabling code, prototyping, and workflow automation within the platform.

MCP: Multi-Context Platform, Figma’s architecture that allows AI agents and developers to interact directly with design files and workflows.

AI credit monetization: Revenue model where users consume prepaid units (“credits”) for AI-powered features, introducing usage-based billing for advanced capabilities.

Full Seat: Highest license tier in Figma, providing comprehensive access to all features including AI tools, prototyping, and admin controls.
