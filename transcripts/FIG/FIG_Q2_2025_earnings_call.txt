Sept. 3, 2025

CALL PARTICIPANTS

Co-Founder and Chief Executive Officer — Dylan Field

Chief Financial Officer — Praveer Melwani

Revenue-- $250 million in revenue for fiscal Q2 2025 (ended June 30, 2025), representing 41% year-over-year growth and setting a new quarterly record.

Non-GAAP Operating Margin-- 5% non-GAAP operating margin in fiscal Q2 2025, reflecting profitability during a period of significant investment and product launches.

Adjusted Free Cash Flow Margin-- Adjusted free cash flow margin was 24% in fiscal Q2 2025, primarily driven by strong collections growth.

Gross Margin-- 90% non-GAAP gross margin in fiscal Q2 2025, with management anticipating near-term compression as AI product spending, particularly inference costs, increases.

Net Dollar Retention Rate-- 129% net dollar retention rate among paid customers spending over $10,000 in ARR in fiscal Q2 2025, attributed to seat expansion and renewals under new pricing and packaging models.

Payers by Segment-- Over 11,900 paid customers generated more than $10,000 in ARR, while more than 1,100 paid customers spent over $100,000 in ARR, representing 42% year-over-year growth as of fiscal Q2 2025.

Product Usage Breadth-- More than 80% of customers used two or more products, and two-thirds used at least three products on the unified platform in fiscal Q2 2025.

New Product Launches-- Figma announced the launch of Figma Make, Figma Draw, Figma Sites, and Figma Buzz, doubling the portfolio and expanding addressable use cases.

Acquisitions-- Modify and Payload were acquired in the quarter to enhance visual expression capabilities and developer-focused content management, respectively.

Developer Engagement-- Developers made up approximately 30% of monthly active users in fiscal Q2 2025.

Localization Initiatives-- Figma expanded support to Korean and Brazilian Portuguese, broadening international accessibility.

Cash, Equivalents, and Securities-- Ending balance of $1.6 billion, including $91 million held in a Bitcoin exchange-traded fund, as of fiscal Q2 2025.

Employee and VC Lockups-- Nonexecutive employees may sell up to 25% of vested holdings, and the five largest VC stockholders have staged lockup releases over the next four quarters, with specific percentages unlocked after each report.

Guidance-- Management projects fiscal Q3 2025 revenue between $263 million and $265 million, and fiscal 2025 revenue in the $1.021 billion-$1.025 billion range, implying 37% year-over-year growth at the midpoint.

Full-Year Operating Income-- Full-year operating income is expected to be between $88 million and $98 million under a flexible, opportunity-based investment framework.

Sales and Marketing Trend-- Expense as a percentage of revenue declined to 39% in fiscal Q2 2025 from 46% in fiscal Q2 2024, even during the ramped-up Config event period.

Pricing and Packaging-- The new model is fully deployed for monthly plans, with annual contracts migrating at renewal; the company expects a mid- to high-single-digit growth tailwind from these changes this year.

AI Investment Plans-- Management stated, "we expect margins to come down in the near term as we invest in the long term," referring to major AI products including Figma Make.

Figma(FIG+9.19%)highlighted robust 41% year-over-year revenue growth and solid non-GAAP profitability for fiscal Q2 2025 (ended June 30, 2025), while emphasizing the rapid expansion of its product suite and go-to-market reach. Expanding internationalization and broader use across customer personas were presented as central to the company's evolving growth model and future monetization plans.

Field said, "Figma Make gives anyone the power to visualize an idea in high fidelity and validate a concept with something realistic."

Melwani stated that non-GAAP gross margins are expected to compress in the near term as AI usage grows, with inference spend identified as the largest driver of the sequential margin decline.

Config, the company's flagship community event, was leveraged to launch new offerings, increase user engagement, and support customer expansion.

Figma committed to complementing its seat-based subscription model with an additional consumption model to reflect new product usage patterns.

Management disclosed that CEO Field established a Rule 10b5-1 trading plan, starting in November, which may lead to the sale of up to 3.3% of his total holdings, inclusive of restricted stock units.

INDUSTRY GLOSSARY

MCP (Model Code Protocol): A Figma developer integration server that enables AI tools to generate code informed directly by Figma design context.

ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): The annualized value of recurring revenue from customers, excluding non-recurring fees.

Inference Spend: Cloud computing costs incurred by running generative AI features and models within the product environment.

Rule 10b5-1 Trading Plan: SEC-compliant plan allowing insiders to pre-schedule trades of company stock at predetermined times or prices.
