Revenue-- $274 million, reflecting 38% year-over-year growth and marking a company record for sequential net revenue added forFigma(FIG+9.19%).

Annualized revenue run rate-- Surpassed $1 billion as of quarter-end.

Net dollar retention (NDR)-- Rose to 131% for paid customers with over $10,000 in annual recurring revenue, up two percentage points quarter over quarter.

Payer base expansion-- 540,000 total paid customers at quarter-end, up from nearly 450,000 at the end of Q1, adding over 90,000 paid teams in two quarters.

Large customer cohorts-- Nearly 13,000 paid customers spend over $10,000 in ARR; over 1,250 paid customers spend more than $100,000 in ARR; 140 net adds for $100,000+ customers, up from 88 prior quarter.

Product adoption-- More than 70% of customers use three or more Figma products; 27% quarter-over-quarter increase in multiyear customer deals.

AI and product innovation-- Over 50 new features launched in the quarter, with Figma Make generally available since July, and 30% of $100,000+ ARR customers using Make weekly by September.

International momentum-- International revenue grew 42%, slightly outpacing domestic growth; strategic investments continue globally, including an upcoming India hub opening.

Gross profit and margins-- Gross profit of $237 million with 86% gross margin; non-GAAP operating margin at 12%; adjusted free cash flow margin at 18% for the quarter.

Operating expenses-- On a non-GAAP basis, expenses increased to support AI initiatives, team expansion, and go-public efforts; recognized a large one-time GAAP stock-based compensation expense as liquidity condition on RSUs was satisfied, resulting in a GAAP net loss.

Cash position-- Ended the quarter with $1.6 billion in cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities.

Outlook upward revision-- Raised full-year revenue guidance to $1.044 billion-$1.046 billion (up $22 million at midpoint), and Q4 revenue guidance to $292 million-$294 million, implying 40% and 35% year-over-year growth at respective midpoints; full-year non-GAAP operating income guidance raised to $112 million-$117 million.

Tax rate-- Non-GAAP tax rate decreased from 25% to 10% due to recent legislation, positively impacting Q3 net income and EPS.

Notable acquisition-- Closed the acquisition of Weavee; it will join as Figma Weave, though integration is expected to occur in coming months and is immaterial to near-term guidance.

Integration and partnerships-- Announced new integrations in the quarter with Gemini, GitHub, Notion, Linear, Subbase, and deeper ChatGPT integration focused on FigJam diagramming; monetization discussions ongoing.

Pricing model transition-- About halfway through rollout; anticipated as a mid to high single-digit growth driver for the year, with further benefits expected in 2026 as full-year renewals occur.

Management confirmed continued acceleration in both customer and revenue growth driven by strong AI product adoption and expanded platform usage. Product launches, including more than 50 new features and the general availability of Figma Make, drove a 27% quarter-over-quarter increase in multiyear deals and spurred platform expansion into new customer segments. International revenues outpaced domestic, underlining the importance of recent global investments, while gross margins remained high even as infrastructure and AI-related expenses rose. The company also implemented a reduced non-GAAP tax rate, which increased quarterly profitability, and raised its outlook for both full-year revenue and operating income.

CEO Field emphasized, "AI is the most important technology shift of our lifetimes so far," highlighting management's conviction in Figma's AI-native strategy as a growth lever.

CFO Malwani stated, "Our annualized revenue run rate is now over $1 billion," which signals scale and continued momentum.

Management reported that Flipkart renewed in the quarter, leveraging the platform to launch a new service "three times faster than previous launches," according to Dylan Field, reflecting sticky enterprise adoption of integrated workflows.

Specific mention was made of 30% of $100,000+ ARR customers using Figma Make weekly by September, indicating early but impactful AI-driven engagement among large accounts.

Recent product integration with ChatGPT and other leading platforms demonstrates Figma's commitment to open ecosystem connectivity and future revenue opportunities, though current contribution is immaterial.

"We are investing to support our product innovation and growth across talent, AI, and M&A," management noted, aligning higher spend with long-term platform leadership.

Industry glossary

Annual recurring revenue (ARR): Total value of recurring revenue from subscriptions over a one-year period, providing a baseline for business predictability and scale.

Net dollar retention (NDR): Metric representing how much recurring revenue from existing customers increases or declines over a specific period, inclusive of upgrades, downgrades, and churn.

RSU (restricted stock unit): Equities granted to employees that vest over time or upon achieving specific milestones; upon vesting, they become tradable shares, and their expensing affects reported earnings.

GA (general availability): Stage when a product is released for mainstream use after beta or limited release phases.

Dev Mode: Figma's product feature designed to help developers inspect and implement design files directly, often integrating with code repositories.

MCP server: Internal Figma technology that synchronizes design context and assets across products and into code, streamlining developer workflows.
