Sales growth-- 15% increase in company sales for the first nine months, with U.S. operations up 15% and international operations up 16%.

Operating profit-- Grew by 10% at constant exchange rates; adjusted operating profit rose 21% at constant exchange rates when excluding restructuring costs.

Guidance update-- Sales growth outlook for 2025 narrowed to 8%-11% at constant exchange rates; operating profit expected at 4%-7% at constant exchange rates due to lower GLP-1 growth expectations and one-off restructuring costs.

GLP-1 diabetes sales-- GLP-1 sales in diabetes rose 10%, with Ozempic and Rybelsus driving growth; Region China GLP-1 diabetes sales decreased 4% due to wholesaler inventory movements.

Obesity care-- Obesity franchise sales climbed 41%, led by U.S. operations at 24% and international operations at 83%, with Wegovy reaching DKK 20 billion and 168% growth; Wegovy prescriptions in U.S. declined from 280,000 to 270,000 weekly versus last quarter.

Rare disease segment-- Rare disease sales rose 13%, driven by 14% growth in the U.S. and 13% internationally.

Restructuring and cost actions-- Company-wide transformation announced with expected reduction of 9,000 positions globally, targeting DKK 8 billion in annual savings by end of 2026; DKK 9 billion restructuring costs booked in the quarter.

Gross margin-- Decreased to 81.0% from 84.6% due to DKK 3 billion one-off restructuring costs and impairments.

Free cash flow-- Reported at DKK 63.9 billion for the first nine months, down from DKK 71.8 billion last year, attributed to increased capital expenditures.

Return to shareholders-- DKK 53 billion returned, mainly via dividends, in first nine months.

Metsera acquisition proposal-- Proposal includes $62.2 cash per share upfront (approximate $6.7 billion enterprise value), with up to $2.8 billion in contingent value rights for milestones, total eligibility up to $10 billion.

Akero acquisition-- Akero’s efruxifermin, a once-weekly FGF21 analog, is being acquired to address unmet needs in F4 MASH patients, supported by SYMMETRY Phase IIb trial data.

Pipeline progress-- Cagrilintide Phase III initiated, REIMAGINE and REDEFINE trials for CagriSema ongoing; regulatory submission for Mim8 in U.S./EU; first readout of ziltivekimab expected in the second half of 2026.

Strategic shift-- CEO Doustdar outlined the sharp focus on diabetes, obesity, and related comorbidities, with non-core research assets discontinued and resources redirected to core areas.

Market leadership-- Novo Nordisk holds a 68% volume market share in GLP-1 in international operations, with expanding availability of Rybelsus (over 40 countries) and Ozempic (about 80 countries).

U.S. market developments-- Management cited growth of compounded GLP-1 usage to over 1 million patients in the U.S., with 10% of Wegovy prescriptions now in the cash market, and ongoing expansion of direct-to-patient initiatives with partners like GoodRx and Costco.

Patent and pricing update-- Management disclosed semaglutide patent expiry in certain countries in 2026 is anticipated to have a low single-digit negative impact on global sales growth, and acceptance of Medicare Part D MFP for Ozempic, Rybelsus, and Wegovy beginning in 2027.

Capital expenditure guidance-- 2025 capital expenditure expected to be around DKK 60 billion; free cash flow guidance at DKK 20 million-30 billion, reflecting updated CapEx and anticipated acquisition timing.

Restructuring will reduce approximately 9,000 positions globally, with DKK 9 billion in associated costs booked and an explicit acknowledgment of the human impact.

Gross margin contracted to 81.0% from 84.6%, largely due to DKK 3 billion in one-off restructuring costs and production-asset impairments.

Management expects 2026 sales growth to be negatively affected by semaglutide patent expiry in certain markets, described as having a "low single-digit" impact on global sales growth.

"Several states have already announced changes to coverage for obesity medicines in response to budgetary concerns, which will affect Medicaid access to Wegovy," as stated by Moore.

Wegovy weekly prescriptions in the U.S. declined sequentially. Management noted that compounding of GLP-1s is "now estimated to be well above 1 million patients in the U.S." undermining authentic product sales.

Management confirmed intensifying competition and pricing pressure in both diabetes and obesity internationally and in the U.S. Lower expectations for GLP-1 sales growth are driving the reduced 2025 guidance.

Novo Nordisk(NVO+0.14%)delivered 15% sales growth in the first nine months, but narrowed its full-year guidance and emphasized headwinds in GLP-1 diabetes and obesity products, reflecting both increased competition and coverage challenges. Capital allocation priorities were reiterated following major acquisition proposals for Metsera and Akero, with the Metsera offer structured for $6.7 billion upfront and up to $10 billion with milestones, while Akero’s lead asset targets a currently untreatable MASH population. Restructuring is a key focus, with 9,000 positions to be eliminated and DKK 8 billion in annual savings projected, although this incurred significant current-period costs and will reshape Novo Nordisk’s operational model and R&D prioritization. Regulatory submissions and near-term launches—including the Wegovy pill and new rare disease assets—are moving forward. Management signaled that pricing, patent expirations, and evolving payer dynamics in the U.S. Medicaid and Medicare programs could materially influence revenue growth through 2026 and beyond.

Metsera’s pipeline, if acquired, would bring differentiated GLP-1 and amylin assets, complementing internal development and widening treatment options by dosing, patient phenotype, and delivery channel.

Efruxifermin, from the Akero acquisition, demonstrated statistically significant benefits in Phase IIb for F4 MASH patients and is positioned for pivotal Phase III readout and possible class leadership in late-stage disease.

REDEFINE and REIMAGINE series, including CagriSema and cagrilintide programs, anchor next-generation obesity and diabetes clinical milestones, with major readouts—such as Wegovy pill approval and Alzheimer’s data—expected before mid-2026.

Management attributed declining market share and modest U.S. prescription trends to intensifying competition, market access friction, and expanding compounded GLP-1 use. "Millions" of untreated diabetes and obesity cases are still viewed as addressable by product and distribution innovation.

The company expects a negative "low single-digit" impact on global sales growth in 2026 from semaglutide patent expiry in select markets, with additional downward pressure from Medicare pricing to commence in January 2027.

Guidance for 2025 capital expenditure stands at DKK 60 billion, with free cash flow projected in the DKK 20 million-30 billion range depending on acquisitions; financial impacts from the proposed Metsera deal were not incorporated in current guidance.

Management remains committed to maintaining its dividend payout strategy alongside R&D expansion and M&A, clarifying that, "have a consistent approach on dividend and have no intention of changing that."

Industry glossary

GLP-1: Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists, a class of drugs used in diabetes and obesity treatment.

MASH: Metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis, a progressive liver disease formerly known as NASH.

FGF21 analog: Fibroblast growth factor 21 analog, a therapeutic protein targeting metabolic diseases like NASH/MASH.

PNH: Paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria, a rare blood disorder treated in the company’s rare disease pipeline.

MFP: Maximum Fair Price under the U.S. Inflation Reduction Act, dictating Medicare Part D drug pricing.

CVR: Contingent Value Right, a future payment right conditional on milestones in an acquisition deal.
